# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Quality Assurance Lead

### Role Summary
Quality Assurance Leads own test strategies and QA planning for projects. They coordinate testing efforts, verify deliverables meet acceptance criteria, and report on quality metrics to ensure high-quality releases.

### Responsibilities
- Own test strategies and QA planning for projects
- Plan and coordinate manual and automated testing efforts
- Verify that deliverables meet acceptance criteria
- Coordinate regression testing and release testing activities
- Report on quality metrics and test coverage
- Identify quality risks and gaps early in the development cycle

### Goals
- Ensure high-quality releases
- Minimize production defects
- Maintain comprehensive test coverage

### Typical Communication
- QA handoffs and bug reports with Developers
- Testing window scheduling with Project Manager
- Requirements alignment and acceptance criteria clarification with Product Manager

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, remove impediments, and coach the team on agile practices. They track team metrics and foster a collaborative, high-performing team environment.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate agile ceremonies (daily standups, sprint planning, retrospectives, sprint reviews)
- Remove impediments and blockers for the team
- Coach the team on agile practices and continuous improvement
- Track and report on team velocity and burndown metrics
- Foster psychological safety and team collaboration
- Shield the team from external disruptions

### Goals
- Maximize team productivity
- Enable self-organization
- Promote continuous improvement culture

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and agile ceremonies with the entire team
- Coordination with Developers, Project Manager, Product Manager, and cross-functional stakeholders
- Regular retrospectives and team health discussions

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers design wireframes, user flows, and UI mockups to create intuitive user experiences. They conduct user research, validate usability, and ensure accessibility standards are met.

### Responsibilities
- Design wireframes, user flows, and UI mockups
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Validate user experience quality and usability of features
- Collaborate with Product Manager on acceptance criteria for design
- Create and maintain design systems and style guides
- Ensure accessibility standards are met

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences that meet customer needs and business objectives

### Typical Communication
- Vision and requirements discussions with Product Manager
- Design handoff and implementation guidance with Developers
- User flow and visual quality validation with QA

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts gather and clarify business requirements from stakeholders, producing detailed user stories and acceptance criteria. They bridge communication between business stakeholders and technical teams.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and clarify business requirements from stakeholders
- Produce detailed user stories and acceptance criteria
- Support backlog refinement and prioritization activities
- Document business processes and workflows
- Bridge communication between business stakeholders and technical teams
- Validate that delivered solutions meet business needs

### Goals
- Ensure clear, actionable requirements that deliver measurable business value

### Typical Communication
- Product vision alignment with Product Manager
- Requirements gathering with Stakeholders
- Technical feasibility and clarification with Developers
- Test scenario validation with QA
